2. FULL MODEL
-------------

Two-stage model: sensor calibration (Stage A) then physiology (Stage B).

Stage A - Power-law latent + gamma kernel sensor model:
  Latent SaO2(t):
    if t <= t_turn:  latent(t) = S_min + (S_start - S_min) * (1 - (t/t_turn)^p)
    if t > t_turn:   latent(t) = S_min + v_up * (t - t_turn)
    where t_turn = t_end, S_start = B_h (locked)

  Properties: latent(0) = S_start = B_h, latent(t_turn) = S_min.
  For p > 1, derivative at t=0 is zero (natural plateau), descent accelerates.
  p is a global parameter controlling curvature shape.

  Sensor pipeline:
    filtered(t) = GammaKernel(latent, eff_lag, cv)
    pred(t) = B_h + b_s * (filtered(t) - B_h)
    eff_lag_h = max(tau_0 + delta_h, 5.0)

  3 ablation configurations tested:
    Config A: b_s free, CV=0.15  (15 params: 3 global + 4 delta + 2x4 latent)
    Config B: b_s=1 fixed, CV=0.15  (14 params: 2 global + 4 delta + 2x4 latent)
    Config C: b_s free, CV=0.10  (15 params: 3 global + 4 delta + 2x4 latent)

  Loss: Student-t NLL (nu=4, sigma=1) on raw (unclipped) predictions
        + Huber nadir timing penalty (delta=8s, lambda=500) on clipped predictions

  Priors:
    tau_0: LogNormal(log 18, 0.4), lambda=3.125
    b_s: N(1, 0.1), lambda=50 (only for configs A and C)
    p: LogNormal(log 3.5, 0.35), lambda=4.08  ** RECENTERED from log 2 **
    delta_h: StudentT-like shrinkage (sigma=3), lambda=5
    delta zero-sum: lambda=500

  Bounds:
    tau_0: [5, 45], b_s: [0.5, 2.0], p: [1.0, 5.0]
    delta_h: [-20, 20]
    S_min: [30, 100], v_up: [0, 3]

  Optimizer: DE popsize=40, maxiter=4000, seed=42, polish=True

Stage B - Apnea-only physiology (frozen sensor):
  PaO2(t) = PvO2 + (PaO2_0 - PvO2) * exp(-t / tau_washout)
  P50(t) = 26.6 + 0.48 * (PaCO2_0 + k_CO2*t - 40)
  SaO2 = Severinghaus ODC with gamma steepness
  pred(t) = clip(B_h + b_s * (GammaKernel(SaO2, eff_lag, cv) - B_h), 0, 100)

  PvO2 fixed at 25.0 mmHg.
  True-frozen mode: tau_0 AND per-hold deltas frozen from Stage A. No delta re-fitting.
  Weak-lag mode: deltas free with N(delta_stageA, sigma=2s). Diagnostic only.
  gamma bounds: [0.8, 2.0]  ** WIDENED from [0.8, 1.3] **

  10 parameters per config: k_co2, gamma + 4*(tau_washout, paco2_0)


3. CHANGES FROM v7.05
---------------------

1. RV#4 excluded from calibration. Was QC-flagged in v7.05 but still in training,
   causing -43% influence on loss. Now excluded entirely, reported as stress-test only.

2. b_s ablation: 3 configurations tested to determine whether b_s should be free
   or fixed to 1.0, and whether CV is the root cause of the v7.05 b_s=1.75 problem.

3. p prior recentered from LogNormal(log 2, 0.35) to LogNormal(log 3.5, 0.35).
   Profile minimum was at p=4.0 in v7.05; old prior actively pulled away from optimum.

4. gamma bounds widened from [0.8, 1.3] to [0.8, 2.0]. gamma was stuck at 1.30
   upper bound in v7.05 in both frozen-lag and weak-lag modes.

5. True-frozen Stage B: both tau_0 and per-hold deltas are frozen from Stage A.
   v7.05's "frozen lag" still allowed delta re-fitting in Stage B, with deltas
   ranging from -20 to +20 (3/5 hitting bounds). This is now prevented.

6. Weak-lag prior tightened: sigma=2s (was 3s in v7.05). lambda_wl = 1/(2*2^2) = 0.125.

7. Training set reduced from 5 to 4 holds (FRC#2, RV#3, FRC#5, FL#6).
   Parameter count: 15 (A,C) or 14 (B) Stage A, 10 Stage B.
   ~1004 data points / 15 params = ~67 points per param.

14. ANALYSIS
-------------

What worked:

  - The b_s problem is completely resolved. b_s=1.0000 in ALL three configs,
    including both free configs. The v7.05 b_s=1.75 was entirely caused by RV#4
    distorting the global fit. Removing it was the only fix needed.

  - This has major implications: the gamma kernel with CV=0.15 is NOT over-smoothing.
    The baseline-corrected measurement equation (pred = B_h + b_s*(filtered - B_h))
    works correctly with b_s=1.0. No gain compensation is needed.

  - The p prior recentering from LogNormal(log 2) to LogNormal(log 3.5) had minimal
    effect on the actual optimum (p shifted from 3.52 to 3.39) but the prior no
    longer fights the data. The profile minimum shifted from p=4.0 (v7.05) to p=3.0
    (Config B), which is more reasonable for a curvature parameter.

  - All 4 training holds have perfect nadir timing (0.0s error) and R2n >= 0.91.
    The weakest hold is FRC#2 (QC-flagged) at R2n=0.91, which is still good.

  - LOHO-Inference R2n=0.96 (4-hold average), exceeding the 0.90 target by a wide
    margin. All individual holds are above 0.91. The model generalizes well.

  - The tau_0 profile bifurcation is sharper than in v7.05: three distinct loss
    plateaus (tau_0<10: ~28K, tau_0=10-15: ~3500, tau_0>=16: ~1300). This suggests
    three qualitatively different solution families. The optimizer consistently finds
    the best (tau_0~18) family.

  - True-frozen Stage B resolves the "frozen lag isn't actually frozen" problem from
    v7.05. Deltas are genuinely frozen. Physiology parameters are stable.

  - Weak-lag divergence dropped from 9.6% to 2.0-4.5% across configs. The tighter
    prior (sigma=2s) and the removal of RV#4 both contributed.

  - No parameters at bound in Stage A for any config. No floor binding on eff_lag.
    This is a clean model with no forced solutions.

What didn't work:

  - gamma = 2.00 AT BOUND in all 3 configs (even after widening from 1.3 to 2.0).
    The ODC steepness parameter still cannot reach its preferred value. The data
    wants an even steeper ODC than gamma=2.0 provides. This needs further widening
    in v7.07 (try [0.8, 3.0] or [0.8, 4.0]).

  - Stage B nadir timing for FRC#2 is +17.0s across all configs. The physiology
    model cannot reproduce the early nadir of this QC-flagged hold. This is a
    Stage B issue (Stage A timing is perfect at 0.0s).

  - Delta range = 15.5s is still fairly wide (RV#3 at -8.0, FL#6 at +7.5). The
    single tau_0 + per-hold delta approach works but requires substantial per-hold
    adjustment. This is inherent in the data (different lung volumes create different
    circulatory delays) and may not be reducible without a covariate model.

  - RV#4 is completely unfittable. R2n = -1.17 with S_min pushed to 99.0 (baseline).
    The post-apnea model cannot represent intra-apnea nadirs. RV#4 should remain
    permanently excluded from calibration.

  - FL#6 tau_washout = 250.0 (at upper bound). This outlier in Stage B ICs is
    consistent across all configs and suggests the physiology model is still
    misspecified for very long holds (FL#6 has t_end=372s).

Key insights:

  1. The entire v7.05 b_s=1.75 problem was caused by a single outlier hold.
     This is a cautionary tale about training with structurally incompatible data.
     RV#4's nadir-during-apnea regime forced the model to compensate globally,
     inflating b_s for all holds. The fix was exclusion, not model complexity.

  2. b_s can be safely fixed to 1.0 and removed from the parameter vector. This
     simplifies the model (2 free global params instead of 3) with zero loss in
     fit quality. The "sensor gain" interpretation is clean: the gamma kernel
     correctly attenuates the latent deviation from baseline.

  3. CV=0.15 vs CV=0.10 makes almost no difference when b_s is at 1.0. Config C
     (CV=0.10) is slightly worse on all metrics (loss: 1328 vs 1241, LOHO: 0.957
     vs 0.961). The narrower kernel (CV=0.10, std=1.8s) is sharper but doesn't help.
     CV=0.15 (std=2.7s) provides enough smoothing without over-attenuating.

  4. The gamma bound is the remaining structural issue. gamma has been at its upper
     bound in every version since v7.04 (1.23 at [0.8,1.3], then 1.30 at [0.8,1.3],
     now 2.00 at [0.8,2.0]). Each widening fills immediately. The physiology model
     needs more ODC steepness than the Severinghaus equation provides, likely because
     the actual ODC in vivo differs from the textbook curve, or because gamma is
     compensating for other unmodeled effects.

  5. The model is now structurally sound for its 4-hold training set. All success
     criteria pass except gamma at bound. The sensor model (Stage A) is
     essentially solved: b_s=1 (fixed), tau_0=17.7s, p=3.4, CV=0.15, identifiable
     profiles, excellent LOHO-Inference generalization.

Recommended next steps:

  - Widen gamma further to [0.8, 3.0] or [0.8, 4.0] to find where gamma stabilizes.
    If it keeps hitting the bound, investigate whether the Severinghaus ODC itself
    needs replacement (e.g., Kelman or Hill equation).

  - Consider profiling CV in {0.08, 0.10, 0.12, 0.15, 0.20} with the finalized
    model (b_s=1, RV#4 excluded) to confirm CV=0.15 is optimal.

  - Package the winning model (Config B) as the "v7 sensor model" for downstream
    use: b_s=1 (fixed), CV=0.15 (fixed), tau_0 and p fit from Stage A, per-hold
    delta and latent params inferred per new hold.

  - Investigate FL#6's tau_washout=250 (at bound). This could indicate that long
    holds need a different washout model (e.g., bi-exponential).
