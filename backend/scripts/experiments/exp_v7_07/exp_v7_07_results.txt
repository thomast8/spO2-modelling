2. FULL MODEL
-------------

Two-stage model: sensor calibration (Stage A) then physiology (Stage B).
Config B only (winner from v7.06): b_s=1 fixed, CV=0.15.

Stage A - Power-law latent + gamma kernel sensor model:
  Latent SaO2(t):
    if t <= t_turn:  latent(t) = S_min + (S_start - S_min) * (1 - (t/t_turn)^p)
    if t > t_turn:   latent(t) = S_min + v_up * (t - t_turn)
    where t_turn = t_end, S_start = B_h (locked)

  Sensor pipeline (simplified with b_s=1):
    filtered(t) = GammaKernel(latent, eff_lag, cv=0.15)
    pred(t) = filtered(t)    [since B_h + 1*(filtered - B_h) = filtered]
    eff_lag_h = max(tau_0 + delta_h, 5.0)

  14 parameters: 2 global (tau_0, p) + 4 delta + 2x4 latent (S_min, v_up)

  Loss: Student-t NLL (nu=4, sigma=1) on raw predictions
        + Huber nadir timing penalty (delta=8s, lambda=500) on clipped predictions

  Priors:
    tau_0: LogNormal(log 18, 0.4), lambda=3.125
    p: LogNormal(log 3.5, 0.35), lambda=4.08
    delta_h: StudentT-like shrinkage (sigma=3), lambda=5
    delta zero-sum: lambda=500

  Bounds:
    tau_0: [5, 45], p: [1.0, 5.0], delta_h: [-20, 20]
    S_min: [30, 100], v_up: [0, 3]

  Optimizer: DE popsize=40, maxiter=4000, seed=42, polish=True

Stage B - Apnea-only physiology (frozen sensor):
  PaO2(t) = PvO2 + (PaO2_0 - PvO2) * exp(-t / tau_washout)
  P50(t) = 26.6 + 0.48 * (PaCO2_0 + k_CO2*t - 40)
  SaO2 = Severinghaus ODC with gamma steepness
  pred(t) = clip(GammaKernel(SaO2, eff_lag, cv=0.15), 0, 100)

  PvO2 fixed at 25.0 mmHg.
  True-frozen mode: tau_0 AND per-hold deltas frozen from Stage A.

  v7.07 changes to Stage B:
    gamma bounds: [0.8, 4.0]  (was [0.8, 2.0] in v7.06)
    gamma prior: N(1.5, 0.5), lambda=2.0  (was N(1, 0.15), lambda=22.2)

  10 parameters: k_co2, gamma + 4*(tau_washout, paco2_0)


3. CHANGES FROM v7.06
---------------------

1. Config B only (b_s=1 fixed, CV=0.15). v7.06 proved this is the winner; no need
   to re-test configs A and C.

2. Gamma bounds widened from [0.8, 2.0] to [0.8, 4.0]. In v7.06, gamma=2.0 was at the
   upper bound in all 3 configs.

3. Gamma prior relaxed from N(1, 0.15) [lambda=22.2] to N(1.5, 0.5) [lambda=2.0].
   The v7.06 prior was too tight and centered too low, actively fighting the data's
   preferred value of gamma >= 2.0.

4. Gamma profile likelihood: fix gamma on a grid of 9 values [0.8..4.0], refit all
   other Stage B params at each point (no gamma prior in profile). This reveals the
   raw loss landscape.

5. FL#6 washout diagnostic: run Stage B with and without FL#6, and with widened
   tau_washout bounds (FL: 250 -> 500), to test whether FL#6's tau_washout=250 at-bound
   is distorting global physiology estimates.

6. Seed robustness check: run Stage A with seeds {42, 123, 456} to confirm the optimizer
   converges to the same basin regardless of initialization.

  k_co2 change (no FL#6):     188.2%
  gamma change (wide washout):  22.6%

FL#6 tau_washout with widened bounds: 376.9 (was 250.0 at bound with standard bounds).
Even with bounds widened to 500, gamma drops from 2.40 to 1.86. This confirms FL#6
is structurally different and is pulling gamma up and k_co2 down.

Analysis: FL#6 is the longest hold (t_end=372s). Its single-exponential PaO2 decay
model is inadequate for such long durations. The optimizer compensates by:
  - Increasing gamma (steeper ODC) to make the model more sensitive to PaO2 changes
  - Decreasing k_co2 (slower CO2 rise) to avoid overshooting at long durations
  - Pushing tau_washout to the bound to slow the PaO2 washout

Without FL#6, gamma=1.89 and k_co2=0.13 are much more physiologically plausible.
The "true" gamma for the 3-hold model is around 1.9, which is close to the v7.06
bound of 2.0 - explaining why gamma was pegging in v7.06.

12. ANALYSIS
------------

What worked:
  - Gamma problem is resolved for the full 4-hold model. Widening bounds from [0.8, 2.0]
    to [0.8, 4.0] and relaxing the prior from N(1, 0.15) to N(1.5, 0.5) allows gamma to
    find its interior optimum at ~2.4.
  - The gamma profile clearly shows a non-monotone shape with minimum at gamma=2.5.
    This confirms the Severinghaus+gamma parameterization is adequate; a Hill-form ODC
    is not needed.
  - Seed robustness is perfect. The Stage A loss surface has a single dominant basin.
  - The two-stage decomposition is clean (weak-lag divergence < 5%).

What didn't work:
  - FL#6 is distorting gamma and k_co2. When FL#6 is excluded, gamma drops from 2.4
    to 1.9 and k_co2 triples from 0.05 to 0.13. This is a large effect.
  - FL#6 tau_washout is at bound (250) even in the free-fit model. Widening to 500
    gives tau_washout=377 but still distorts gamma (1.86).
  - The single-exponential PaO2 decay model is structurally inadequate for FL#6's
    372-second hold duration.
  - FRC#2 has poor nadir-window R2 (0.46) due to recovery timing, but apnea R2 is good.

Comparison: v7.06 vs v7.07
  Metric                    | v7.06 (Config B) | v7.07 (4 holds) | v7.07 (no FL#6)
  --------------------------+------------------+-----------------+----------------
  gamma                     | 2.00 *BOUND*     | 2.405           | 1.895
  k_co2                     | (at bound regime)| 0.046           | 0.134
  gamma at bound            | YES              | NO              | NO
  FL#6 tau_w at bound       | YES (250)        | YES (250)       | N/A
  Stage B loss              | ~1241            | 3753            | 2781

Note: v7.07 losses are not directly comparable to v7.06 because the gamma prior
changed (lambda 22.2 -> 2.0), which reduces regularization and allows higher raw loss.

Key insights:
  1. The gamma problem was caused by two factors working together:
     (a) Bounds too tight [0.8, 2.0] cutting off the optimum at ~2.4
     (b) Prior too tight N(1, 0.15) pulling gamma toward 1.0
     Either fix alone would have partially helped; both together fully resolve it.

  2. FL#6 is a separate structural problem. It needs either:
     (a) A bi-exponential PaO2 decay model for long holds, or
     (b) Exclusion from Stage B calibration (use for validation only), or
     (c) A hold-specific gamma (not recommended - loses parsimony)

  3. The "true" gamma for short/medium holds is ~1.9 (from the 3-hold model).
     FL#6 pushes it up to ~2.4. Both are in a physiologically reasonable range
     (steeper-than-standard ODC, consistent with Bohr effect during prolonged apnea).

  4. k_co2 = 0.13 (3-hold) vs 0.05 (4-hold) is a large difference. The 3-hold value
     is more physiologically plausible (CO2 rises ~8 mmHg/min during apnea, so
     k_co2 = 0.13 mmHg/s = 7.8 mmHg/min, consistent with literature).


13. RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS
---------------------------

1. v7.08: Investigate FL#6 treatment.
   Option A: Bi-exponential PaO2 decay for FL-type holds (adds 2 params per FL hold).
   Option B: Exclude FL#6 from Stage B calibration, use 3-hold model (gamma~1.9, k_co2~0.13).
   Option C: Test with FL#6 tau_washout uncapped (bounds [50, 1000]) to see if it stabilizes.
   Recommendation: Start with Option B (simplest, most principled). FL#6 becomes a
   validation/stress-test hold like RV#4.

2. Update gamma prior to N(2.0, 0.5) [lambda=2.0] centered at the empirical optimum
   from the 3-hold model (1.9 rounds to 2.0).

3. Consider profiling k_co2 in the 3-hold model to check its identifiability.

4. Do NOT switch to Hill-form ODC. The gamma profile proves the Severinghaus+gamma
   parameterization has an interior minimum. The ODC is fine.
